1: What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?
The first thing I noticed when interacting with the experience was the large imagery on the right hand side depicting MyoungAe Kim’s portfolio works. My eyes were immediately drawn there because of the sheer size of the works, and the usage of gifs and slideshow images. Those two aspects contrasts against the slim text and lines that remained static when just simply scrolling down the page.
2: Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.
  • Watched the first gif of ‘Advent Calendar-like Web Portfolio’ play out.
  • Scrolled up and down to view the overall experience.
  • Clicked through bits and pieces of the gifs and slideshow images showing design work.
  • Clicked the boxes on the left-hand side because of the text saying “click the boxes to explore”.
  • Moved the contents, the cakes, of the boxes around to see what they did. Then clicked on them rapidly because the hand wasn’t changing back to an arrow.
  • Found a box that had “Merry Christmas” in it, pressed it which turned the website red.
  • Flickered the colours of the website between red and white using “Merry Christmas”.
  • Clicked on the top hyperlink ‘Advent Calendar-like Web Portfolio’, taking me to a portfolio page.
  • Went through their separate works using the bottom left index page, skimming through the text and images of each one.
3: What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?
I spent the most time clicking through the advent calendar because it had multiple actions that kept me on it. The action of opening a Christmas calendar on a website interested me and kept me wondering what could possibly be in the next box. The 3D cakes, tree, and wreath found in the boxes had pops of green and red that went along with the Christmas theme, which stood out against the flat and colourless background. Being able to click and drag these assets kept me engaged because I wanted to see what they could do. Then the box with “Merry Christmas” in it, kept me stuck for a few seconds simply because flickering the website’s colour scheme was a simple and repetitive action that changed such a large part of the website.
4: What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?
The most common action I took was clicking on different items; such as looking through the items in the advent calendar, or looking at the slideshow images and gifs showcasing the designer’s works.
5: What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?
The primary goal of the interactive experience seems to be showing the designer’s works, and depicting their values and personal style through the website to appeal to potential employers. The designer, MyoungAe Kim, wants so stand out from the crowd to be more employable by showing their skillset, and therefore their value as an employee.
6: How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?
The interactive experience is stylized to have a minimal black and white aesthetic throughout, which indicates Kim’s either preference or strengths in that style. But since it’s minimalistic, it creates more focus on the imagery taking up half the screen that shows off her works so it quickly grabs the attention of employers. The interaction of clicking through the advent calendar also makes the website interesting, showing employers that she’s creative.
7: What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?
The experience doesn’t seem like it would be interacted with for a long time and seems to be a one off interaction unless being gone back to for inspiration by other designers; timing would be around 3 minutes when skimming through her work descriptions and clicking through the advent calendar. But if someone was truly invested into her work because they’re thinking of hiring Kim or wants to understand where parts of her work are justified, they’d spend more time reading through paragraphs which could take around 8 minutes.
8: How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?
The experience doesn’t have any mechanisms that would bring a user back regularly; it is overall a portfolio of MyoungAe Kim’s work so it effectively showcases different designs she has made, but other than the advent calendar bringing in interest for someone to take inspiration from, people generally wouldn’t stay or come back to the website.
9: What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?
The most obvious connection is the advent calendar style interaction on the landing page, where boxes can be opened to discover different small items within them. The scrollable area showing snippets of her work is reminiscent of a historical timeline, where there’s a line that has branches extending from it to show different years and events indicated by the dots on the side of each title.
10: What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?
Creating an advent calendar through basic grids guides the user to click through the boxes since people generally do have personal experiences with advent calendars. Then mimicking a historical timeline encourages users to see the designer’s history of works, see her past works, and improvement throughout projects.
11: What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?
The minimalistic visual style is quite calming as it doesn’t have too much going on throughout the page. Yet overall, the simplicity of the website stimulates interest by creating focus through large boxy images of Kim’s portfolio pieces. This clear layout would make users feel satisfied with the interaction as it’s swift in delivering the necessary information of Kim’s goal, being to showcase her work.
